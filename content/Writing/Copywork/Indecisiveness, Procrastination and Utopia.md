For an nth time, I planned to chase the competitive programming dream again.   

Candidate Master, I shall become -  I said.  

"Sabse behtar banuga mein"... the pokemon intro played in my head, I clenched my fist in the air, focussed my eyes into an imagined horizon and my brain flooded with grit and optimism.

But...   

This is the word that comes as an omen, as a cameo of a person turning into a full-fledged villain in the upcoming few episodes.

"But, I will need a system to manage my preparation"  

Sounded fair.  

"How about I build an app for that?"  

So subtle was the jab that was about to cause concussion, and put me into coma. Few minutes later, I was scrambling through Youtube to come with an inspiration for the look, and the tech that I would be using to build the app. Two days later, I had drawn a sketch for the UI, discussed vehemently with AI and have exhausted many hours in ideation, imagining an ideal workflow where everything went seamlessly and flawlessly.  

"What would be the best UI, what latest tech should I use to build, cloud tools, best practices..." - I have been travelling through a rabbit hole, all under the pretext of doing work.  

"Why don't you use what's convenient for you? You need it for yourself, right? No one is checking under the hood. No one is blaming for bad code."  

I realised this after two days of being serious. God knows how late the realisation would have come if I had remained casual. The greatest hurdle is to listen to your ego and then dismiss it. Clarity will only come through striving.
