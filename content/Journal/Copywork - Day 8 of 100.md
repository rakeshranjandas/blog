# Ignore Sunk Costs

**Author** - Seth Godin  
**Source** - https://seths.blog/2009/05/ignore-sunk-costs/  

### Summary

```
- Sunk costs are irrelevant
- Example 1: land for gas station. (Money is sunk)
- Example 2: tickets for concert. (Time is sunk)
```

The author talks about how investments made in the past are irrelevant while making decisions for the future. For example, while deciding to develop a gas station, the location of the lands should be considered and the amount spent on acquiring those lands should not matter. Only the benefit of the decision in the future, not what has been done in the past, should matter.
