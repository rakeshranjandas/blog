# Advice For Authors

**Author** - Seth Godin  
**Source** - https://seths.blog/2006/08/advice_for_auth/  


### Summary

```
Advice for authors
- Lower your expectations
- Start promoting 3 years prior to release
- Hiring an editor is worth it
- A non-fiction book works to spread your ideas
- Target a small, selected audience
- Don't hire a publicist
- Book cover design matters
- Blog mentions are better than blurbs
- Personal conversations with people for promotion are worthwhile
- Consider free PDF release if you dont want any hassle
- Publishing and printing are different things
- Bookstores are unreliable
- Do write a book
```

The authors gives a list of do's and don't for new authors to be successful with their books in the current market. Most of the ideas revolve around being more empathetic and taking full ownership and, mostly, keeping it real. Promoting a book has to be planned way ahead of time so that the fan-base is already built before the book comes out. Keeping expectations grounded by focusing on a small group, meeting them in person and relying on them to spread the popularity is a better strategy than aiming to make it grand by spending tons of time and money on a high-end publisher. But hiring an editor is worth investing as they help keep the emotions of the book in line. The author even asks to new authors to consider publishing for free to build credibility without compromising on the real objective of creating the book in the first place, which is to spread ideas and opinions, and force the reader to think.


### How I felt

This post was bigger than the previous ones. So it took 2 days to copywork.

Some learnings - 
- using `First` is better than `Firstly`.
	-e.g. `Firstly, this wont happen. Secondly, it's unfair`.
	Better version - `First, this won't happen. Second, it's unfair`. 
- using `to` instead of `so that`
	-e.g. `I practiced hard so that I could pass the exam`. 
	Better version - `I practiced hard to pass the exam`
